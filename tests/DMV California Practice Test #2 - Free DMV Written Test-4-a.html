28.
To see vehicles in your blind spots, you should check:
The inside rearview mirror
The outside rearview mirror
Over your shoulders
Explanation ▾
By definition, blind spots are areas that cannot be seen using your mirrors. Therefore, you should look over your shoulders to check them.
29.
If you must park your vehicle in an area not usually used for parking:
Park with your back-up lights on.
Make sure your vehicle is visible to drivers approaching from either direction.
Park 5 feet from the curb.
Explanation ▾
Drivers are responsible for making sure their vehicles do not become a hazard after they have been parked. Wherever you park, make sure your car is visible to drivers approaching from either direction.
30.
A peace officer is signaling you to drive to the edge of the roadway. You decide to ignore the officer's warning and flee the scene. You are guilty of a misdemeanor and can be punished by being:
Fined up to $1,000
Jailed in the county jail for not more than one year
Given a warning and a citation
Explanation ▾
Any person, while operating a motor vehicle, who willfully flees or attempts to evade a peace officer performing his or her duties is guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by imprisonment in a county jail for not more than one year.
31.
By law, the brakes on every vehicle (other than a motorcycle) must:
not lock under any condition
consist of a good foot brake and an "emergency" or "parking" brake
consist of a good "parking" brake that is capable of stopping the vehicle within legal limits
Explanation ▾
Tennessee law requires that all automobiles have both a regular foot brake and a parking brake.
32.
To avoid glare from headlights of an approaching car:
Keep looking briefly to the side of the road
Stay focused on the center line on the road
Flash your high beams
Explanation ▾
You should avoid looking toward the bright lights of an oncoming car by glancing towards the right side of the road, and then looking ahead to check on the position of the other vehicle.
33.
You see a flashing yellow traffic signal at an upcoming intersection. The flashing yellow light means:
Stop before entering the intersection as long as you can do so safely.
Stop. Yield to all cross traffic before crossing the intersection.
Slow down and cross the intersection carefully.
Explanation ▾
A flashing yellow traffic signal light warns you to proceed with caution. Slow down and be alert before entering the intersection. Yield to any pedestrians, bicyclists, or vehicles in the intersection.
34.
The flashing yellow light at an intersection means you should:
Slow down and proceed with caution
Come to a complete stop as quickly as possible
Stop and proceed only when clear
Explanation ▾
When encountering a flashing yellow light at an intersection, you should slow down and proceed with caution. You do not need to come to a complete stop when approaching a flashing yellow light.
35.
If you plan to pass another vehicle, you should:
Not assume the other driver will make space for you to return to your lane.
Assume the other driver will let you pass if you use your turn signal.
Assume the other driver will maintain a constant speed.
Explanation ▾
Even when you signal, you should not assume that the space you want to occupy is free or that other drivers will give you the right of way.
36.
You want to make a right turn at the corner. A pedestrian with a guide dog is at the corner ready to cross the street in front of you. Before making your right turn, you should:
Turn off your engine until the person crosses the street
Tell the pedestrian when to cross the street
Wait until the person crosses the street
Explanation ▾
Pedestrians using guide dogs or white canes (with or without a red tip) must be given the right-of-way at all times.
